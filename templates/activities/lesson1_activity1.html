A phrasal verb is a combination of words (a verb + a preposition or verb + adverb) that when used together, usually take on a different meaning to that of the original verb. Check out a few examples:

  • Ella tore up the letter after she read it.
  • Their car broke down two miles out of town
  • We left out the trash for pickup.

We often use verbs with the following words to create phrasal verbs:
In, on, up, away, about, over, by, out, off, down, back, through, along, forward.
